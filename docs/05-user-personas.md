# User Personas

## Persona 1: Fresh Graduate
### Ahmed, 22

**Background:**
- Just graduated from FAST-NUCES
- CGPA: 3.0
- Studied Computer Science
- No professional experience (only internships)
- Lives in Lahore with parents

**Goals:**
- Get first job as a Junior Developer
- Earn PKR 70,000+ per month
- Work in a reputable software house
- Learn and grow professionally

**Pain Points:**
- Doesn't know if his resume is good enough
- Applied to 50+ companies, no callbacks
- Afraid of technical interviews
- No industry connections
- Doesn't know which role to target
- Can't afford expensive career coaches

**What He Needs:**
- Resume scoring to understand weaknesses
- Role-specific guidance (which role to target?)
- Interview practice to reduce anxiety
- Confidence boost from seeing progress
- Affordable (free or cheap)

**Behavior:**
- Uses phone and laptop equally
- Active on LinkedIn
- Watches YouTube tutorials daily
- Prefers visual content over text

**Frustrations:**
- "Why am I not getting callbacks?"
- "What should I write in my resume?"
- "How do I prepare for interviews?"

## Persona 2: Career Switcher
### Sana, 28

**Background:**
- Chemical Engineer by education
- Self-taught Python and Data Science
- Worked as a process engineer for 4 years
- Now wants to transition to Data Analytics
- Lives in Karachi, married

**Goals:**
- Transition from chemical engineering to data analytics
- Earn PKR 120,000+ per month
- Work remotely
- Build a strong portfolio

**Pain Points:**
- How to present non-CS background effectively?
- Which data skills are most in-demand?
- Can't afford expensive bootcamps or coaches
- Needs portfolio guidance
- No CS degree (worried about discrimination)
- Resume gets rejected due to non-CS background

**What She Needs:**
- Role-specific resume optimization
- Targeted skill recommendations
- Realistic career roadmap
- Affordable guidance
- Confidence in her skills

**Behavior:**
- Uses laptop primarily
- Active on LinkedIn and GitHub
- Takes online courses (Coursera, Udemy)
- Reads tech blogs

**Frustrations:**
- "Will companies even consider me without a CS degree?"
- "How do I show my transferable skills?"
- "Am I wasting my time?"

---

## Persona 3: Working Professional
### Bilal, 35

**Background:**
- 10 years in IT
- Currently Senior Developer (backend) at a software house
- Team Lead on paper but wants official title
- Lives in Islamabad, family of 4

**Goals:**
- Move to Team Lead/Senior Architect role
- Earn PKR 250,000+ per month
- Manage bigger teams
- Reduce coding, focus on architecture

**Pain Points:**
- Resume is outdated (last updated 5 years ago)
- Leadership interviews are different from technical ones
- Need to highlight management skills
- How to quantify achievements?
- No time for long-form learning
- Need quick, actionable feedback

**What He Needs:**
- Executive-level resume optimization
- Leadership and system design interview practice
- Progress tracking for career development
- Quick, actionable insights

**Behavior:**
- Works 10+ hours daily
- Uses laptop exclusively
- Very busy, needs efficiency
- Prefers reading over videos (faster)
- Values certifications

**Frustrations:**
- "How do I write an executive-level resume?"
- "Am I ready for a leadership role?"
- "How do I showcase my management skills?"

## Design Implications

### For Ahmed (Fresh Graduate)
- Simple, encouraging UI
- Clear action buttons ("Start Now," "Upload Resume")
- Tooltips and guides for beginners
- Progress bars and achievements for motivation
- Mobile-friendly (uses phone)

### For Sana (Career Switcher)
- Explanatory tooltips ("Why do you need this?")
- Role-specific guidance
- Testimonials from career switchers
- Realistic career roadmap
- Portfolio building tips

### For Bilal (Working Professional)
- Professional, data-rich dashboard
- Executive-level language
- Metrics and KPIs prominently displayed
- Quick actions (minimal clicks)
- Desktop-focused design

### Our UI Must Cater to ALL Three
- Clean, professional (LinkedIn-inspired)
- Not too simple (Bilal will feel it's childish)
- Not too complex (Ahmed will feel overwhelmed)
- Tooltips for beginners
- Advanced options for professionals
- Modular design (show/hide complexity)
